Process

A structured path from first request to the right presentation support.

Process is where the diagnostic-first relationship becomes explicit: request, intake, review, reply with the best next step, then move into refinement or rehearsal only where it will materially help.

01 / Request

Request the diagnostic.

Raise a hand with the real presentation, not a hypothetical future project.

02 / Intake

Submit the presentation context.

Share the audience, timing, stakes, and whether the friction is message, deck, delivery, or some combination.

03 / Review

Get the material reviewed.

The review starts with the current script, slides, or recorded delivery.

04 / Reply

Receive the best next step.

The recommendation may point to refinement, rehearsal, sprint support, coaching, or no further help if that is the honest answer.

Locked Wording

Submit the request and we'll review the presentation context, then reply with the best next step.

What to Send

Bring the current presentation in, not a vague summary of it.

The intake should make it easy to understand what is being reviewed and why the moment matters.

Script or speaking notes

Use this when the message, structure, or emphasis still needs work.

Slide deck

Use this when the deck is part of the problem: hierarchy, pacing, density, or credibility.

Recorded delivery

Use this when timing, transitions, presence, or rehearsal quality is the main concern.

Recommendation Logic

The process should make the right level of support obvious.

The goal is not to force every request into the same package. The goal is to match the need honestly.

When the next step is focused support

  • Move into message or slide refinement when the content itself needs improvement.
  • Move into rehearsal when the material is close but delivery needs work.
  • Move into a sprint when the stakes are high and the timeline is tight.

When the honest answer is lighter

  • Recommend a smaller adjustment if the presentation is fundamentally in good shape.
  • Point to toolkit or practice guidance when a full engagement is unnecessary.
  • Keep booking as follow-up when appropriate, not the first mandatory step.
