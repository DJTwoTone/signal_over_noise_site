Process

A clear path from first request to the right presentation support.

The diagnostic comes first: request, context, review, then a recommendation that matches the actual need.

01 / Request

Request the diagnostic.

Start with the real presentation, not a hypothetical future project.

02 / Context

Submit the presentation context.

Share the audience, timing, stakes, and whether the friction is in the message, the deck, the delivery, or some mix of the three.

03 / Review

Get the material reviewed.

The review starts with the current script, slides, or recorded delivery.

04 / Recommendation

Receive the best next step.

The recommendation may point to refinement, rehearsal, sprint support, coaching, or no further help if that is the honest answer.

What to Send

Bring the current presentation, not a vague description of it.

The intake should make it easy to understand what is being reviewed and why it matters.

Script or speaking notes

Use this when the message, structure, or emphasis still needs work.

Slide deck

Use this when the deck is part of the problem: hierarchy, pacing, density, or credibility.

Recorded delivery

Use this when timing, transitions, presence, or rehearsal quality is the main concern.

Recommendation Logic

The process should make the right level of support obvious.

The goal is not to push every request into the same package. The goal is to match the need honestly.

When the next step is focused support

  • Move into message or slide refinement when the content itself needs work.
  • Move into rehearsal when the material is close but delivery needs attention.
  • Move into a sprint when the stakes are high and the timeline is tight.

When the honest answer is lighter

  • Recommend a smaller adjustment if the presentation is already in decent shape.
  • Point people to the toolkit or practice guidance when a full engagement is unnecessary.
  • Keep booking as a follow-up step when it makes sense, not the first hurdle.
