# Client_Strategy_Brief

## Project Summary
Signal over Noise needs a workshop-ready, mobile-first marketing site that converts interest into leads quickly without feeling overbuilt. The site should support two connected funnels: a low-friction workshop QR flow that captures emails through a useful toolkit, and a higher-intent presentation diagnostic flow that leads into consults and paid services.

This is a premium, editorial-feeling multi-page site designed to feel credible, sharp, and easy to act on. v1 should launch fast, stay easy to update, and use placeholders where needed so momentum is never blocked.

## Business Goal
Generate qualified leads for Signal over Noise, then move those leads toward:
- Presentation cleanup / refinement work
- Coaching engagements
- Presentation sprints
- Future custom workshops and team training

## Audience
### Primary audience for v1
Professionals presenting in English and multilingual settings.

### Expanded audience framing
Experts and teams presenting to international, multilingual, or mixed-language audiences.

### Audience positioning rule
Do not frame the audience as deficient, struggling, or linguistically inadequate. Frame the problem around presentation stakes, clarity, structure, and delivery in multilingual communication contexts.

## Offer
### Primary free workshop offer
**Signal over Noise Presenter Toolkit**

Toolkit assets:
- Script Development Worksheet
- Slide Review Checklist
- Presentation Rehearsal Plan

### Secondary free higher-intent offer
**Free Presentation Diagnostic**
- Request-based, not the first ask for workshop QR visitors
- Includes a short PDF review before a short consult
- Diagnostic types:
  - Slide Deck Diagnostic
  - Script Diagnostic
  - Video Performance Diagnostic

## Primary CTA + Secondary CTA
### Site-wide default
- **Primary CTA:** Request a Free Presentation Diagnostic
- **Secondary CTA:** Get the Signal over Noise Presenter Toolkit

### Workshop QR funnel
- **Primary CTA on /scan:** Get the Signal over Noise Presenter Toolkit
- **Secondary CTA on /scan:** Request a Free Presentation Diagnostic

### Thank-you page
- **Primary CTA on /thanks:** Book / Request Your Free Presentation Diagnostic
- Secondary actions may include exploring services or returning to the site

## Traffic / Acquisition Context
### v1 traffic sources
- Workshop QR codes
- Direct link sharing
- Referral traffic
- General site visitors

### Conversion logic
Workshop traffic should not be pushed directly into a heavy consult-first flow. Instead:
1. QR scan lands on `/scan`
2. Visitor claims toolkit via light email capture
3. Visitor is redirected to `/thanks`
4. `/thanks` promotes the Free Presentation Diagnostic
5. Diagnostic flow leads toward consult and paid services

## Positioning / Differentiation
### Core positioning
Signal over Noise helps professionals present with more clarity, confidence, and credibility in English and multilingual settings.

### Differentiation direction
Signal over Noise focuses on:
- Stronger message structure
- Sharper slides
- Better rehearsal and delivery
- High-stakes presentation support
- A premium, design-aware approach that connects story, slides, and performance

### Brand distinction
The brand should feel editorial, high-contrast, institutional, and precise, not playful, soft, or startup-cute.

## Messaging Direction
### Strategic messaging rule
Lead with presentation outcomes and working context, not language shame.

### Messaging themes
- Clearer presentations
- Stronger structure
- Sharper slides
- Better rehearsal
- More confidence in English and multilingual presentation settings
- Professional support for high-stakes communication

### Audience language rule
Use a mix of:
- “professionals presenting in English and multilingual settings”
- “experts and teams presenting to international, multilingual, or mixed-language audiences”

## Key Objections to Address
- “Is this for me, or only for people in a very specific market?”
- “Do I need coaching, or do I just need help fixing a deck?”
- “Will this feel judgmental about my English?”
- “Is the diagnostic actually useful, or just a sales pitch?”
- “What happens after I submit?”
- “How customized is the support?”
- “Do I need to commit to a big package right away?”
