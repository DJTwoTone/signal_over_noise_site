# Signal over Noise — Pencil Final Update Instructions

## Purpose
This document gives the next-pass update instructions for the current Signal over Noise website design in Pencil.

It is based on the current approved direction:
- premium editorial / civic / high-contrast visual system
- diagnostics-first main site
- toolkit-first workshop QR flow
- stronger proof, stronger hierarchy, fewer placeholder-feeling sections
- restrained design with selective visual drama, not decorative clutter

Use this as the working build brief for the next design pass.

---

## What is locked

### Core homepage headline
**Make your next presentation clearer, sharper, and more credible.**

### Core homepage subhead
**Signal over Noise helps professionals presenting in English and multilingual settings strengthen the message, refine the slides, rehearse the delivery, and prepare for high-stakes moments.**

### Primary CTA label
**Request a Free Presentation Diagnostic**

### Secondary CTA label
**Get the Signal over Noise Presenter Toolkit**

### Core audience framing
Use this tone across the site:
- professionals presenting in English and multilingual settings
- experts and teams presenting across international and mixed-language contexts

Avoid language that sounds condescending, remedial, or “we fix your English.”

---

## Design intent for this pass
Do **not** redesign the site from scratch.

Keep the current structure that is already working:
- hero
- audience/context
- pathways
- process
- proof
- featured offers
- final CTA band

The job now is to make the current design:
- more persuasive
- less placeholder-like
- less repetitive
- more editorial in rhythm
- more believable through proof

---

## Global Pencil rules

### Keep
- cream page background as the main field
- deep blue for major anchors, panels, nav CTA, and final CTA bands
- gold only as a selective accent
- serif display headlines
- clean sans-serif body text
- ruled lines, spacing, and hierarchy doing most of the work

### Avoid
- turning every section into equal cards
- using the same layout rhythm over and over
- large gold blocks
- generic business stock image vibes
- repeated filler body copy
- giant paragraphs explaining things the UI can show faster

### Push more
- stronger type contrast
- clearer hierarchy between sections
- more asymmetry in key moments
- better proof framing
- more distinct CTA posture from page to page
